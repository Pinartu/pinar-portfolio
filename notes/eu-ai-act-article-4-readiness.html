EU AI Act · Article 4

You're 7 weeks from the EU AI Act's literacy deadline. Here's the self-audit I'd run first.

By Pinar Taskin · Legal → AI & E-Commerce · Published Jun 16, 2026 · 4 min read

We're less than seven weeks from August 2, 2026 — and most companies aren't ready for what changes that day.

What changes

From Aug 2, 2026, AI literacy becomes a legal obligation. Under Article 4 of the EU AI Act, everyone who uses AI on your company's behalf must be trained to use it responsibly.

Does this apply to us?

It reaches far beyond the EU. Article 4 names no list of countries. Wherever your headquarters sits, if the AI systems you build, distribute or use interact with the EU market — directly, or indirectly through their outputs — your company carries the AI literacy obligation.

And it's not just full-time staff. Interns. Consultants. Contractors. Anyone using AI for your business is in scope.

The gap

Most companies have no idea where they stand. No tool inventory. No training records. No written AI policy.

Where to start

From a legal background, now building AI into e-commerce daily, here's the self-audit I'd run first. Ask your team these questions — honest answers only.

Section 1 · AI Inventory & Awareness

  1. Is there an up-to-date inventory of all AI tools used across the department?
  2. Do you have a mechanism to detect Shadow IT (unregistered AI usage)?
  3. Is it defined in writing which data must never be entered into AI systems?

Section 2 · Training Programs (Article 4)

  1. Has basic AI literacy training been provided to all staff?
  2. Are trainings role-specific (e.g. bias for HR, hallucinations for Finance)?
  3. Is there training on detecting false information (hallucinations)?
  4. Does training cover copyright, data privacy and ethical use?
  5. Are external providers & contractors included in the training scope?

Section 3 · Documentation, Evidence & Monitoring

  1. Are training attendance records and curricula archived?
  2. Has the internal AI policy been acknowledged by all staff?
  3. Is AI training built into the onboarding process?
  4. Is there a plan to update the trainings periodically?

The takeaway

Count your "No"s. That's your roadmap for the next seven weeks. Which section would your company struggle with most? Save this and run it with your team.
